Yi-Chung Chen received the B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering-nanotechnology from Yuan Ze University, Taoyuan, Taiwan, in 2006, the M.S. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Polytechnic School of Engineering, New York University in 2011, and the Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Swanson school of Engineering, University of Pittsburgh in 2014. He is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Tennessee State University, Nashville, TN. His research interests include HW/SW co-design, domain-specific computing, high performance computing, advanced computing system, nonvolatile memory system, finite element method, EDA tool development, ultra low power embedded system, reconfigurable computing, and human brain-inspired AI.
